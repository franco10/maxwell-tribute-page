James Clerk Maxwell

The man who unified electricity, magnetism and light

Time line of James Clerk Maxwell

  • 1831 - Born in Edinburgh, Scotland.

  • 1844 - Wins the mathematical medal and first prize for both English and poetry at the Edinburgh Academy.

  • 1845 - Writes his first scientific paper.

  • 1847 - Begins attending classes at the University of Edinburgh

  • 1849 - Contributes two papers for the Transactions of the Royal Society of Edinburgh.

  • 1850 - Joins University of Cambridge.

  • 1854 - Graduates from Trinity (University of Cambridge) with a degree in mathematics.

  • 1855 - Presents his paper "Experiments on Colour" to the Royal Society of Edinburgh

  • 1859 - Awarded the Â£130 Adams Prize for his essay "On the stability of the motion of Saturn's rings".

  • 1858 - Marries the physical scientist Katherine Mary Dewar.

  • 1860 - Awarded the Royal Society's Rumford Medal.

  • 1861 - Publishes his two-part paper "On physical lines of force".

  • 1865 - Publishes an article entitled "A Dynamic Theory of the Electromagnetic Field" in which the now world famous equations known as "Maxwell's equations" first appeared.

  • 1868 - Publishes his paper "On governors".

  • 1870 - Publishes his paper "On reciprocal figures, frames and diagrams of forces".

  • 1871 - Writes the textbook "Theory of Heat".

  • 1876 - Writes the treatise "Matter and Motion".

  • 1876 - Elected as a member to the American Philosophical Society.

  • 1879 - Dies in Cambridge at the age of 48.
